Wenhui Zhu (朱文辉)

I am a Ph.D. Candidate at the School of Computing and Augmented Intelligence (SCAI), Arizona State University, advised by Prof. Yalin Wang. My research spans across the frontier of Generative AI, focusing on post-training alignment, autonomous agents, and efficient multi-modal learning.

I build long-horizon LLM & VLM agents through mid/post-training (alignment & reinforcement learning) and efficient architectures. Backed by a solid mathematical foundation in Optimal Transport and Generative Frameworks (Diffusion & Schrödinger Bridge), my long-term goal is to build secure, self-evolving agentic systems capable of executing complex open-ended workflows.

My work has been regularly presented at top-tier venues including CVPR, ECCV, ICML, WACV, and IPMI.
